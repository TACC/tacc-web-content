Cybersecurity
CompTIA Security + Certification Virtual Program

Approx. 4 Hours per Week

This virtual, multi-week program is designed to prepare participants to pass the CompTIA Security + exam. The CompTIA Security+ certification, validates the baseline skills necessary to perform core security functions for compensation. The program addresses the need for cybersecurity training across any position (HR, IT, engineering, etc) that uses computers, networks, and devices.

Cybersecurity is the protection of internet-connected systems such as hardware, software and data from cyber-threats. The practice is used by individuals and enterprises to protect against unauthorized access to data centers and other computerized systems.

All past TACC summer camp participants are invited to register. There is no cost for training materials and exam. Coding experience is not required. Participants are expected to participate until the conclusion of the program. Sessions (instruction and review) are offered multiple times during the week and will be recorded for your participation convenience.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the dates of the program?

A: June 22 – September 6, 2020. Participants who complete practice exam will receive an exam code the week of September 6, 2020 to take the CompTIA Security + Certification Exam.

Q: What is the eligibility of the Code@TACC Cybersecurity Certification Program?

A: This program is open only to past TACC summer camp participants.

Q: How can I contact the UT Austin Code@TACC Cybersecurity Certification Program Team?

A: Please email our office at outreach@tacc.utexas.edu or call 512-553-2502.

Q: Where will the program be located?

A: This program will take place over the internet, virtually.

Q: Do I need to have access to Wifi?

A: Participants who notify TACC will have an opportunity to receive a hotspot. Hotspots are limited and will be given out on a first request basis.

Q: Do I have to have my own computer or laptop?

A: Participants can use a windows or mac computer. If participants do not have the necessary equipment such as laptops, they can be loaned from TACC.

Q: Who will be supervising me during the program?

A: Students will be supervised on the calls by college counselors. Counselors have been background checked (also checked through the national sex offender registry) and have received child protection training.

Q: Can I leave the program early?

A: Participants are expected to continue until the end of the program.
